Tamil Literature

Kambar (c. 12th–13th century)

Classical Tamil poet best known for the Kamba Ramayanam, a retelling of the Ramayana in rich, musical Tamil.

Who was Kambar?

Kambar (also spelled Kamban) was a celebrated Tamil poet and scholar from the Chola period. He is renowned for composing the Kamba Ramayanam, an epic that adapts Valmiki’s Ramayana with distinctive Tamil poetics, devotion, and dramatic storytelling.

Major Work

  • Kamba Ramayanam — Epic poem in Tamil, traditionally organized into Baala Kaandam, Ayodhya Kaandam, Araṇya Kaandam, Kishkindha Kaandam, Sundara Kaandam, and Yuddha Kaandam.

Themes & Style

  • Devotion (bhakti) and moral virtue
  • Vivid imagery and musical cadence
  • Elegant metaphors and classical Tamil rhetoric

Why Kambar matters

Kambar’s retelling shaped Tamil cultural memory of the Ramayana and remains a pillar of Tamil literature, studied for its language, aesthetics, and ethical ideals.

Memorable Line (translated)

“With virtue as his crown and compassion in his gaze, he walked the path that guards the world.”

Quick Facts

  • Era: Chola period
  • Language: Tamil
  • Genre: Epic poetry
